## Severity Classification

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Contraindicated** | The combination must not be used. Risk of serious harm or death. |
| **Severe** | The combination may cause significant clinical harm. Avoid if possible; if necessary, use with close monitoring and dose adjustment. |
| **Moderate** | The combination may alter drug effect or increase adverse reactions. Monitor closely and adjust doses as needed. |
| **Mild** | The combination has limited clinical significance. Awareness is recommended but routine management is usually sufficient. |

## 1. Metformin Interactions

### Metformin + Alcohol
- **Severity**: Moderate to Severe
- **Mechanism**: Both metformin and alcohol inhibit hepatic gluconeogenesis. Alcohol also impairs lactate clearance.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of lactic acidosis and hypoglycemia. The risk is greatest with binge drinking or chronic heavy alcohol use.
- **Management**: Advise patients to limit alcohol intake. Avoid excessive or binge drinking. Monitor for symptoms of lactic acidosis (malaise, myalgia, respiratory distress, abdominal pain).

### Metformin + Iodinated Contrast Dye
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: Iodinated contrast media may cause acute kidney injury, impairing metformin clearance and leading to accumulation.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of metformin-associated lactic acidosis (MALA).
- **Management**: Discontinue metformin at the time of or prior to the contrast procedure. Withhold for at least 48 hours after the procedure. Reassess renal function (eGFR) before restarting. This is especially important if the patient has eGFR 30-60 mL/min/1.73m2.

### Metformin + ACE Inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril)
- **Severity**: Mild
- **Mechanism**: ACE inhibitors may improve insulin sensitivity.
- **Clinical Effect**: Potentially additive glucose-lowering effect. Rarely clinically significant.
- **Management**: Monitor blood glucose. No dose adjustment typically required. This combination is commonly prescribed together safely in diabetic hypertensive patients.

### Metformin + Topiramate
- **Severity**: Moderate
- **Mechanism**: Topiramate can cause metabolic acidosis (carbonic anhydrase inhibition), which may increase the risk of metformin-associated lactic acidosis.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of lactic acidosis.
- **Management**: Monitor serum bicarbonate levels. Consider alternative anticonvulsant if metabolic acidosis develops.

## 2. ACE Inhibitor (Lisinopril) Interactions

### Lisinopril + Potassium Supplements / Potassium-Sparing Diuretics
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: ACE inhibitors reduce aldosterone secretion, decreasing potassium excretion. Adding exogenous potassium or potassium-sparing agents compounds this effect.
- **Clinical Effect**: Hyperkalemia, which may lead to cardiac arrhythmias (peaked T waves, widened QRS, ventricular fibrillation).
- **Management**: Avoid routine potassium supplementation in patients on ACE inhibitors. If potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, eplerenone) are required (e.g., for heart failure), monitor serum potassium within 1 week of initiation and regularly thereafter. Target potassium < 5.0 mEq/L.

### Lisinopril + NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen, naproxen)
- **Severity**: Moderate
- **Mechanism**: NSAIDs inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, which reduces renal blood flow and blunts the antihypertensive and renoprotective effects of ACE inhibitors.
- **Clinical Effect**: Reduced antihypertensive efficacy, increased risk of acute kidney injury, and hyperkalemia. The "triple whammy" of ACE inhibitor + NSAID + diuretic is particularly nephrotoxic.
- **Management**: Use the lowest effective NSAID dose for the shortest duration. Monitor blood pressure, renal function, and potassium. Consider acetaminophen as an analgesic alternative.

### Lisinopril + Lithium
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: ACE inhibitors reduce renal lithium clearance by enhancing proximal tubular reabsorption of sodium and lithium.
- **Clinical Effect**: Lithium toxicity (tremor, ataxia, confusion, renal impairment, seizures).
- **Management**: If combination is necessary, reduce lithium dose and monitor serum lithium levels frequently (weekly initially, then monthly). Consider ARB as an alternative (similar interaction risk exists but may be slightly lower).

### Lisinopril + Aliskiren
- **Severity**: Contraindicated (in diabetic patients)
- **Mechanism**: Dual RAAS blockade.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of hyperkalemia, hypotension, and renal impairment. The ALTITUDE trial demonstrated increased adverse events with no benefit.
- **Management**: Do not combine in patients with diabetes or eGFR < 60.

## 3. Statin Interactions

### Statins + Grapefruit Juice
- **Severity**: Moderate (applies primarily to simvastatin, lovastatin, atorvastatin)
- **Mechanism**: Grapefruit inhibits intestinal CYP3A4, increasing statin bioavailability.
- **Clinical Effect**: Elevated statin levels increase the risk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis.
- **Management**: Avoid large quantities of grapefruit juice (> 1 quart/day) with simvastatin or lovastatin. Atorvastatin is less affected. Rosuvastatin and pravastatin are not metabolized by CYP3A4 and are safe with grapefruit.

### Statins + CYP3A4 Inhibitors (e.g., clarithromycin, itraconazole, protease inhibitors)
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: Strong CYP3A4 inhibition dramatically increases plasma levels of CYP3A4-metabolized statins.
- **Clinical Effect**: Markedly increased risk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis.
- **Management**: Avoid simvastatin and lovastatin with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. Use lower doses of atorvastatin (max 20 mg). Rosuvastatin or pravastatin are preferred alternatives. Temporarily suspend statin therapy during short courses of strong inhibitors.

### Statins + Fibrates (e.g., gemfibrozil, fenofibrate)
- **Severity**: Severe (gemfibrozil); Moderate (fenofibrate)
- **Mechanism**: Gemfibrozil inhibits statin glucuronidation (via OATP1B1 and UGT enzymes), increasing statin exposure. Fenofibrate has a different metabolic profile and lower interaction risk.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis, especially with gemfibrozil.
- **Management**: Avoid gemfibrozil + statin combinations. If a fibrate is needed with a statin, use fenofibrate. Monitor for muscle symptoms (pain, weakness, dark urine). Check CK if symptomatic.

## 4. Amiodarone Interactions

### Amiodarone + Warfarin
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: Amiodarone inhibits CYP2C9 and CYP3A4, the major enzymes responsible for warfarin metabolism. This interaction has a prolonged onset (weeks) due to amiodarone's long half-life (40-55 days).
- **Clinical Effect**: INR elevation of 30-50% on average, significantly increasing bleeding risk.
- **Management**: Reduce warfarin dose by 30-50% when initiating amiodarone. Monitor INR weekly for the first 2-3 months, then monthly. The interaction may persist for months after amiodarone discontinuation.

### Amiodarone + Digoxin
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: Amiodarone inhibits P-glycoprotein-mediated renal clearance of digoxin and may also reduce its volume of distribution.
- **Clinical Effect**: Digoxin levels may increase by 70-100%, leading to toxicity (nausea, visual disturbances, bradycardia, arrhythmias).
- **Management**: Reduce digoxin dose by 50% when initiating amiodarone. Monitor digoxin levels within 1-2 weeks and titrate accordingly.

### Amiodarone + QT-Prolonging Drugs (e.g., sotalol, fluoroquinolones, ondansetron, antipsychotics)
- **Severity**: Severe to Contraindicated
- **Mechanism**: Additive prolongation of the QT interval through potassium channel blockade.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of torsades de pointes, a potentially fatal polymorphic ventricular tachycardia.
- **Management**: Avoid combination with other QT-prolonging agents whenever possible. If necessary, obtain baseline ECG with QTc measurement, monitor QTc regularly, maintain serum potassium > 4.0 mEq/L and magnesium > 2.0 mg/dL.

### Amiodarone + Statins (simvastatin, lovastatin)
- **Severity**: Severe
- **Mechanism**: Amiodarone inhibits CYP3A4, increasing statin exposure.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis.
- **Management**: Simvastatin dose must not exceed 20 mg/day with amiodarone. Lovastatin should not exceed 40 mg/day. Preferably use rosuvastatin or pravastatin, which are not CYP3A4 substrates.

## 5. Empagliflozin (SGLT2 Inhibitor) Interactions

### Empagliflozin + Insulin or Sulfonylureas
- **Severity**: Moderate
- **Mechanism**: Additive glucose-lowering effect.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of hypoglycemia.
- **Management**: Consider reducing insulin or sulfonylurea dose by 20-30% when adding empagliflozin. Monitor blood glucose closely during initiation.

### Empagliflozin + Loop Diuretics (e.g., furosemide)
- **Severity**: Moderate
- **Mechanism**: Additive diuretic and natriuretic effects. SGLT2 inhibitors promote osmotic diuresis via glucosuria.
- **Clinical Effect**: Increased risk of volume depletion, dehydration, hypotension, and acute kidney injury.
- **Management**: Assess volume status before initiation. Consider reducing diuretic dose if the patient is already euvolemic. Monitor blood pressure, renal function, and electrolytes. Advise patients to maintain adequate fluid intake.

### Empagliflozin + ACE Inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril)
- **Severity**: Mild to Moderate
- **Mechanism**: Both agents can reduce blood pressure and affect renal hemodynamics.
- **Clinical Effect**: Potential for additive hypotension. However, this combination is generally beneficial in diabetic patients (complementary cardiorenal protection).
- **Management**: Monitor blood pressure, especially during initiation. The combination is commonly used and well-tolerated. Watch for orthostatic symptoms, particularly in elderly patients or those on multiple antihypertensives.

### Empagliflozin + Metformin
- **Severity**: Mild
- **Mechanism**: Complementary glucose-lowering mechanisms without pharmacokinetic interaction.
- **Clinical Effect**: Enhanced glycemic control. Available as a fixed-dose combination product.
- **Management**: No dose adjustment required. Monitor for adverse effects of each drug individually. This combination is standard practice in T2DM management.
