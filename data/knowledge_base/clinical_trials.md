## Trial 1: EMPA-REG OUTCOME

**Full Title**: Empagliflozin Cardiovascular Outcome Event Trial in Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Patients - Removing Excess Glucose

**Publication**: Zinman B, et al. Empagliflozin, Cardiovascular Outcomes, and Mortality in Type 2 Diabetes. N Engl J Med. 2015;373:2117-2128.

### Population
- 7,020 patients with Type 2 diabetes mellitus and established cardiovascular disease.
- Mean age 63 years; 72% male; mean HbA1c 8.1%; mean BMI 30.6 kg/m2.
- 77% on metformin, 48% on insulin, 43% on sulfonylureas at baseline.
- 81% on RAAS inhibitors, 77% on statins, 65% on aspirin.

### Intervention
- Patients randomized to empagliflozin 10 mg (n=2,345), empagliflozin 25 mg (n=2,342), or placebo (n=2,333).
- Median follow-up: 3.1 years.
- Background glucose-lowering therapy was adjusted by investigators as needed.

### Key Findings
- **Primary composite endpoint** (cardiovascular death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke): 10.5% empagliflozin vs. 12.1% placebo (HR 0.86, 95% CI 0.74-0.99, p=0.04).
- **Cardiovascular death**: 3.7% vs. 5.9% (HR 0.62, 95% CI 0.49-0.77, p<0.001) - a 38% relative risk reduction.
- **Hospitalization for heart failure**: 2.7% vs. 4.1% (HR 0.65, 95% CI 0.50-0.85, p=0.002) - a 35% relative risk reduction.
- **All-cause mortality**: 5.7% vs. 8.3% (HR 0.68, 95% CI 0.57-0.82, p<0.001) - a 32% relative risk reduction.
- No significant difference in non-fatal MI or non-fatal stroke individually.
- Genital infections were more common with empagliflozin (6.4% vs. 1.8%).

### Conclusion
In patients with T2DM and established cardiovascular disease, empagliflozin added to standard care significantly reduced the risk of cardiovascular death, hospitalization for heart failure, and all-cause mortality. These benefits were observed early and were consistent across subgroups. This trial was pivotal in establishing SGLT2 inhibitors as a preferred second-line therapy for T2DM patients with cardiovascular disease.

### Relevance to the Clinical Scenario
The sample patient (Jan K., 58M with T2DM, HTN, and chest pain under evaluation) may benefit from empagliflozin if cardiovascular disease is confirmed, given the cardiovascular mortality benefit demonstrated in this trial.

## Trial 2: SYNERGY-AF (Synthetic)

**Full Title**: SGLT2 Inhibition and Antiarrhythmic Therapy in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes and Atrial Fibrillation: A Randomized Controlled Trial

**Note**: This is an entirely fictional/synthetic trial created for workshop demonstration purposes.

### Population
- 2,450 patients with T2DM and paroxysmal or persistent atrial fibrillation.
- Mean age 65 years; 60% male; mean HbA1c 7.8%.
- All patients on a stable antiarrhythmic regimen (amiodarone 55%, flecainide 25%, sotalol 20%).
- 82% on metformin, 70% on RAAS inhibitors, 68% on statins.

### Intervention
- Patients randomized to empagliflozin 10 mg daily (n=1,225) or placebo (n=1,225), added to existing antiarrhythmic therapy.
- Median follow-up: 2.5 years.

### Key Findings
- **AF recurrence** (primary endpoint): 28.3% empagliflozin vs. 34.7% placebo (HR 0.78, 95% CI 0.67-0.91, p=0.002).
- **Composite of cardiovascular death or heart failure hospitalization**: 8.2% vs. 12.1% (HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.51-0.86, p=0.003).
- **New-onset heart failure**: 4.1% vs. 6.9% (HR 0.59, 95% CI 0.42-0.83, p=0.003).
- Empagliflozin was associated with modest reduction in left atrial volume index at 12 months (-3.2 mL/m2 vs. -0.8 mL/m2, p=0.01).
- In the amiodarone subgroup (n=1,349), empagliflozin showed a trend toward reduced amiodarone-related thyroid events (4.2% vs. 5.8%, p=0.12), though this was not statistically significant.
- No significant pharmacokinetic interaction between empagliflozin and amiodarone was identified. No increased risk of QT prolongation.
- Genital infections were more frequent with empagliflozin (5.8% vs. 1.5%).

### Conclusion
In patients with T2DM and atrial fibrillation on antiarrhythmic therapy, the addition of empagliflozin reduced AF recurrence and cardiovascular events. The mechanism may involve favorable atrial remodeling through reduced left atrial volume, inflammation, and fibrosis. SGLT2 inhibitors may represent a useful adjunctive therapy in this population, with a favorable interaction profile alongside common antiarrhythmics including amiodarone.

## Trial 3: DIABSTAT (Synthetic)

**Full Title**: Statin Therapy for Cardiovascular Risk Reduction in Diabetic Patients Presenting with Non-Cardiac Chest Pain: A Randomized Outcome Trial

**Note**: This is an entirely fictional/synthetic trial created for workshop demonstration purposes.

### Population
- 3,800 patients aged 45-75 with T2DM who presented with chest pain, underwent cardiac workup (ECG, troponin, stress testing), and were found to have no evidence of acute coronary syndrome or obstructive coronary artery disease.
- Mean age 59 years; 55% male; mean HbA1c 7.5%; mean LDL 138 mg/dL.
- 75% on metformin; 60% on RAAS inhibitors; none on statins at enrollment.
- 42% had hypertension; 18% had evidence of non-obstructive coronary plaque on CCTA.

### Intervention
- Patients randomized to atorvastatin 20 mg daily (n=1,900) or placebo (n=1,900).
- Median follow-up: 4.2 years.

### Key Findings
- **Primary composite endpoint** (first occurrence of cardiovascular death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or coronary revascularization): 6.8% atorvastatin vs. 10.4% placebo (HR 0.64, 95% CI 0.52-0.79, p<0.001).
- **Non-fatal MI**: 2.1% vs. 4.0% (HR 0.52, 95% CI 0.36-0.74, p<0.001).
- **Mean LDL reduction**: 39% from baseline in the atorvastatin group vs. 2% in the placebo group.
- Subgroup analysis: Patients with non-obstructive plaque on baseline CCTA had the greatest benefit (HR 0.48, 95% CI 0.31-0.74).
- Patients on concurrent metformin showed no pharmacokinetic interaction with atorvastatin.
- Muscle-related adverse events: 3.2% atorvastatin vs. 2.8% placebo (p=0.48). No cases of rhabdomyolysis.
- New-onset diabetes was not a factor (all patients already had T2DM).

### Conclusion
In diabetic patients presenting with chest pain who were found not to have ACS, initiation of moderate-intensity statin therapy significantly reduced the risk of future major cardiovascular events over 4 years. The benefit was greatest in patients with non-obstructive coronary plaque detected on initial workup. This trial supports guideline recommendations that virtually all diabetic patients aged 40-75 should receive statin therapy for primary prevention, even in the absence of acute coronary disease at initial presentation.

### Relevance to the Clinical Scenario
The sample patient (Jan K., 58M with T2DM and chest pain, not currently on a statin) is precisely the type of patient who would benefit from statin initiation regardless of the stress test result, consistent with the DIABSTAT findings and current diabetes management guidelines.
