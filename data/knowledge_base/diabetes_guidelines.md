## 1. Diagnostic Criteria

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) is diagnosed when any one of the following criteria is met on two separate occasions (unless unequivocal hyperglycemia is present):

- **HbA1c >= 6.5%** (48 mmol/mol), performed in a laboratory using a method certified by the NGSP and standardized to the DCCT assay.
- **Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) >= 126 mg/dL** (7.0 mmol/L). Fasting is defined as no caloric intake for at least 8 hours.
- **2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL** (11.1 mmol/L) during an oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) using a 75g glucose load.
- **Random plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL** (11.1 mmol/L) in a patient with classic symptoms of hyperglycemia or hyperglycemic crisis.

Prediabetes is identified by an HbA1c of 5.7-6.4%, fasting glucose of 100-125 mg/dL, or 2-hour OGTT glucose of 140-199 mg/dL. Patients with prediabetes should be counseled on lifestyle modifications and monitored annually.

## 2. Treatment Goals

### Glycemic Targets

- **HbA1c < 7.0%** (53 mmol/mol) is appropriate for most non-pregnant adults. This target has been shown to reduce microvascular complications.
- **HbA1c < 6.5%** may be considered for select patients with short disease duration, long life expectancy, and no significant cardiovascular disease, if achievable without significant hypoglycemia.
- **HbA1c < 8.0%** may be appropriate for patients with a history of severe hypoglycemia, limited life expectancy, advanced complications, or extensive comorbid conditions.

### Blood Glucose Targets (Self-Monitoring)

- Preprandial (fasting) capillary glucose: **80-130 mg/dL** (4.4-7.2 mmol/L).
- Peak postprandial capillary glucose (1-2 hours after the start of a meal): **< 180 mg/dL** (10.0 mmol/L).

## 3. Pharmacological Treatment

### First-Line Therapy

**Metformin** remains the recommended first-line pharmacological agent for T2DM in the absence of contraindications:

- Starting dose: 500 mg once or twice daily with meals.
- Titrate gradually (every 1-2 weeks) to reduce gastrointestinal side effects.
- Maximum effective dose: 2000 mg/day (commonly given as 1000 mg BID).
- Extended-release formulations may improve GI tolerability.
- **Contraindicated** when eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2. Dose reduction recommended when eGFR is 30-45 mL/min/1.73m2. Should not be initiated if eGFR < 45.
- Hold before and 48 hours after iodinated contrast procedures.

### Second-Line and Add-On Therapy

When metformin monotherapy does not achieve target HbA1c within 3-6 months, add a second agent based on patient-specific factors:

- **SGLT2 Inhibitors** (e.g., empagliflozin, dapagliflozin): Preferred in patients with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD), heart failure, or chronic kidney disease. Provide cardiovascular and renal benefits independent of glucose lowering. Notable side effects include genital mycotic infections and euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis.
- **GLP-1 Receptor Agonists** (e.g., semaglutide, liraglutide): Preferred in patients with established ASCVD or high cardiovascular risk. Offer weight loss benefits. Injectable formulations; oral semaglutide also available.
- **DPP-4 Inhibitors** (e.g., sitagliptin, linagliptin): Weight-neutral, well tolerated. Do not combine with GLP-1 receptor agonists.
- **Sulfonylureas** (e.g., glipizide, glimepiride): Effective and inexpensive but carry risk of hypoglycemia and weight gain.
- **Thiazolidinediones** (e.g., pioglitazone): Effective but associated with fluid retention, weight gain, and fracture risk. Avoid in heart failure.
- **Insulin therapy**: Consider when HbA1c > 10%, fasting glucose > 300 mg/dL, or if the patient is symptomatic. Basal insulin (e.g., glargine, degludec) is typically initiated first.

## 5. Complications Screening

### Retinopathy
- Dilated eye examination at the time of T2DM diagnosis and annually thereafter.
- If no retinopathy and good glycemic control, screening may be extended to every 2 years.

### Nephropathy
- Annual measurement of urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) and serum creatinine with estimated GFR (eGFR).
- Microalbuminuria (UACR 30-300 mg/g) warrants initiation or optimization of ACE inhibitor or ARB therapy.
- Macroalbuminuria (UACR > 300 mg/g) requires aggressive management and nephrology referral consideration.

### Neuropathy
- Comprehensive foot examination at least annually, including inspection, pulse assessment, and 10-g monofilament testing.
- Screen for symptoms of peripheral neuropathy (numbness, tingling, pain) at each visit.
- Assess for autonomic neuropathy symptoms including orthostatic hypotension, gastroparesis, and erectile dysfunction.

### Cardiovascular Risk Assessment
- Assess cardiovascular risk factors annually (lipids, blood pressure, smoking status).
- Statin therapy is recommended for virtually all diabetic patients aged 40-75 years.
- Aspirin (75-162 mg/day) for secondary prevention in patients with known ASCVD.

## 6. Lifestyle Modifications

Lifestyle interventions are the foundation of T2DM management and should be reinforced at every visit:

- **Medical nutrition therapy**: Individualized meal planning emphasizing whole grains, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Carbohydrate counting or consistent carbohydrate intake can help with glucose management. Limit added sugars and refined carbohydrates.
- **Physical activity**: At least 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking), spread over at least 3 days per week with no more than 2 consecutive days without activity. Include resistance training 2-3 sessions per week.
- **Weight management**: For overweight or obese patients, aim for 5-10% body weight reduction. Even modest weight loss significantly improves insulin sensitivity and glycemic control.
- **Smoking cessation**: Strongly recommended. Smoking increases cardiovascular risk and worsens diabetic complications.
- **Alcohol**: Moderate consumption (up to 1 drink/day for women, up to 2 for men) is acceptable if glucose is well controlled. Alcohol can increase the risk of hypoglycemia, especially with insulin or sulfonylureas. Excessive alcohol should be avoided, particularly in patients taking metformin (increased lactic acidosis risk).
- **Psychosocial support**: Screen for diabetes distress, depression, and anxiety. Refer for behavioral health support as needed.
